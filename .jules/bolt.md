## 2024-05-20 - [Avoid Sqrt and Large Fractional Exponents in Inner Loops]
**Learning:** The molecular dynamics inner loops, particularly in Lennard-Jones force calculations (e.g., in `matlab/LJ_Force.m`), have previously used `sqrt()` to calculate distance and large fractional exponentiation like `^13` or `^7`. This not only created a significant performance bottleneck due to the cost of `sqrt` and power operations, but it also masked a dimensional error in the force vector calculation (multiplying by the distance vector instead of the unit vector, effectively missing a division by $r$).
**Action:** When implementing or optimizing molecular force calculations in this codebase, compute the squared distance ($r^2$) directly using dot products or simple addition of squared components. Derive forces using inverse powers ($1/r^2$, $1/r^6$, etc.) rather than computing distances and fractional exponents. This improves both execution speed and mathematical correctness.
